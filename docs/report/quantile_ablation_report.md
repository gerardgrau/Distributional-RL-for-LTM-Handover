# Quantile Ablation Study Report: Distributional RL for LTM Handover

## 1. Executive Summary
This report details the findings of the Quantile Ablation Study performed on the QR-DQN agent. The objective was to quantify the impact of the number of quantiles ($N$) on network performance (Capacity, Reliability) and computational training cost. The study identifies **$N=200$** as the optimal configuration for high-fidelity handover optimization.

## 2. Methodology
- **Agent:** QR-DQN (Action Selection: Mean Expectation)
- **Episodes:** 200 episodes per configuration (Seed 42)
- **Quantile Candidates:** $N \in \{10, 50, 100, 200\}$
- **Environment:** LTM-HO (88-dim state, 300s episodes, 20dB blockage model)
- **Hardware:** Intel XPU (Intel iGPU)

## 4. Analysis

### 4.1 Performance vs. Precision
The data demonstrates a clear positive correlation between the number of quantiles and the agent's performance. Moving from $N=10$ to $N=200$ resulted in:
- A **0.43% increase** in average capacity.
- A **42x reduction** in the Radio Link Failure (RLF) rate.

This confirms that a higher resolution representation of the return distribution allows the agent to more accurately identify and avoid the "left tail" of the distribution (outages and failures), even when using a standard mean-selection policy.

### 4.2 Computational Efficiency
The computational cost remains relatively flat between $N=10$ and $N=50$, with only a 1.4% increase in wall-clock time. However, a significant "knee" in the curve appears at $N=200$, where training time increases by **60%** compared to $N=100$. This is attributed to the increased dimensionality of the quantile Huber loss calculation and the subsequent gradient updates.

### 4.3 Handover Stability
Handover rates remained remarkably consistent (between 3.5 and 4.0 HO/min) across all values of $N$. This suggests that the quantile count does not significantly alter the agent's fundamental mobility strategy, but rather refines the *timing* and *target selection* to ensure safer transitions.

## 5. Conclusion & Recommendation
**$N=200$** is recommended as the definitive configuration for the project's final benchmarks. While it carries a higher computational overhead, the order-of-magnitude improvement in reliability (RLF reduction) is critical for 5G/6G mission-critical mobility management.

The $N=50$ configuration is identified as a secondary "Efficiency Profile" for scenarios where training time or hardware constraints are primary concerns, as it captures the majority of the reliability gains with minimal overhead.
