## Key Findings

### 1. CVaR truncation is essential
- Without truncation (cvar_full N=100): reward 966.6, hof 4.81 — catastrophic
- With truncation: reward 1066.0, hof 1.47 — best across the board
- Truncating the upper 90% of the head concentrates capacity on the part
  that matters for action selection

### 2. The matched-K paper insight
At matched effective quantile budget (K=25):
- midpoint N=25 (k=25):    reward 1050.1, hof 2.60
- cvar_trunc N=250 α=0.10 (k=25): reward 1021.5, **hof 1.16** (halved)

→ CVaR effect is NOT a quantile-budget artifact. The risk policy
genuinely trades reward for safety.

### 3. α (risk fraction) sweet spot
At N=25, kappa=1.0:
| α    | k  | reward      | hof  |
|------|----|-------------|------|
| 0.05 | 2  | 1019.6      | 1.21 |
| 0.10 | 3  | 1030.4      | 1.15 |
| 0.20 | 5  | 1043.2      | 1.14 |
| 0.30 | 8  | 1046.7      | 1.25 |
| 0.50 | 13 | **1054.5**  | 1.33 |

Wider tail → better reward; α=0.50 (median) is the sweet spot.

### 4. κ (Huber threshold) interacts with α
At α=0.50, N=25:
| κ    | reward      | hof  |
|------|-------------|------|
| 0.25 | **1056.4**  | 1.41 |
| 0.5  | 1046.1      | 1.54 |
| 1.0  | 1054.5      | 1.33 |
| 2.0  | 1045.3      | 1.67 |

κ=0.25 wins reward, κ=1.0 wins hof. Picked κ=0.25 for headline.

### 5. Network capacity (hidden=256) gives big lift on risk policy
ct anchor N=25 α=0.10 κ=1.0:
- hidden=[128,128]: reward 1030.4
- hidden=[256,256]: reward 1047.4 (+17)

Combined with α=0.50+κ=0.25: reward 1066.0 (HEADLINE V2).

### 6. Things that did NOT help
- **midpoint+mean N axis (N=10/25/50/100)**: flat at ~1042-1050. midpoint
  is mathematically DQN when α=1.0/mean.
- **n-step (n=3, n=5)**: HOF nearly doubles for both DQN and QR-DQN.
- **quadrature variation (Simpson, beta_*)**: <1% reward delta.
- **grad_clip=10**: -2.8 reward.
- **adam_eps=1.5e-4**: +5 reward (within noise).
- **DQN HP tuning (lr=3e-4, tau=0.05)**: marginal.
- **cvar_full N=100 (no truncation)**: catastrophic.
- **cvar_trunc N=500**: past peak.
