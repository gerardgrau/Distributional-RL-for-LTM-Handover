## Conclusion (honest, not a clean sweep)

It is **not** a strict Pareto domination — the split is interpretable:

- **QR-DQN wins the safety-critical + objective metrics**: reward, hof
  (nearly halved), rlf, capacity, reliability.
- **DQN wins the activity/overhead metrics**: fewer handovers, lower
  ping-pong, less preparation and resource reservation.

The distributional-RL story: the risk-aware QR-DQN is **more willing to
hand over** (higher ho/prep/res/pp) but its handovers are **far more
reliable** (hof −49%, rlf −39%) and yield higher throughput. DQN is more
conservative (less activity, lower overhead) but its handovers fail ~2×
as often. QR-DQN buys reliability with signalling activity.

vs the **LTM heuristic**: at 2000 ep QR-DQN's hof (1.147) now essentially
**matches** the baseline (1.151) — an improvement over the 1000-ep run
(1.31, worse than baseline) — while beating it on capacity (+5.9%),
reliability (+1.0pt), reward (+2.9%) and ping-pong (−45%). Cost side
unchanged: more handovers and ~30% more resource reservation.

(2.63 → 1.56), so the 2000-ep numbers are the ones to cite for the paper.
